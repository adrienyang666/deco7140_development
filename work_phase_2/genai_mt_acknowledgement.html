GenAI and MT Acknowledgement

Context

This assessment involved creating a multi-page user research and analysis website for the Food and Culinary Communities project. I used ChatGPT to assist with brainstorming content, simulating interviews, and generating/refining HTML/CSS. Grammarly was used to improve grammar and clarity.

AI Usage Log

  • Client Needs Analysis: Helped identify and structure values, needs, and features using flip-card layout and plain English summarising.
  • User Stories: Interviewed personas via ChatGPT and translated their insights into user stories using the required span-tag format.
  • Accessibility Assessment: Generated content suggestions aligned with WCAG 2.2 and rewrote a long list into clear, visual sections.
  • Ethics Analysis: Explored ACS Code applications and structured concerns and solutions into professional sections.
  • GenAI Reflection: ChatGPT helped frame the checklist and reflection text. I revised the content to reflect my experience truthfully.

Checklist Reflection

Tool Selection

Used ChatGPT for ideation, research, and HTML/CSS assistance. Used Grammarly for clarity and proofreading.

Review & Verification

All AI-generated outputs were checked and edited to suit project goals and tone.

Privacy & Ethics

No real data or personal identifiers were used. Personas were fictional. Outputs were checked for cultural bias.

Transparency

This page acknowledges the scope and nature of AI support clearly, following the GenAI and MT Usage Framework.

Reflection

ChatGPT significantly helped in idea generation and content structuring, especially when transforming interview responses into user stories and improving visual clarity in accessibility content. However, the tool sometimes gave generic or repetitive outputs, so I learned to refine prompts and always review the final output myself. I now better understand the importance of using AI tools as support rather than a shortcut.

Integrity Statement

I confirm that all use of AI and MT tools was documented, ethically used, and critically reviewed. This support did not replace original thinking or engagement and is acknowledged transparently.
