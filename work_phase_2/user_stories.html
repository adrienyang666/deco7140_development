Adrien's User Research – Food and Culinary Communities


Introduction

This user research was conducted using Generative AI to simulate interviews with three diverse personas within the Food and Culinary Community. These personas reflect cultural diversity, intergenerational knowledge sharing, and the emotional connections people form through food.


User Stories

Nai Nai Li

Age: 68

Background: Chinese-Australian grandmother living in Brisbane

Need: Share traditional recipes with her family

Insight: Prefers voice and visuals over long text. Emphasizes cultural preservation.

User Story:
As a Chinese grandmother, I want to share my recipes online so our traditions are not lost.

Aiden

Age: 27

Background: Indian-Australian chef running pop-up events

Need: Share fusion recipes and promote food events

Insight: Wants image/video gallery and responsive event listings.

User Story:
As a multicultural chef, I want to share fusion recipes and events to reach more people.

Emilia

Age: 22

Background: Colombian international student in Sydney

Need: Share and find comfort food to reduce homesickness

Insight: Needs emotional connection, multilingual content, simple navigation.

User Story:
As an international student, I want to find and share traditional recipes to feel closer to home.

Summary

These personas highlight the importance of intergenerational storytelling, cultural preservation, emotional comfort, and accessible digital features in food-related web experiences.
