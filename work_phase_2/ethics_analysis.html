Ethics Analysis

Overview

This page outlines key ethical considerations involved in designing a community website for the Food and Culinary Communities, referencing the Australian Computing Society (ACS) Code of Professional Conduct. Each concern is addressed with a corresponding ethical solution aligned with professional standards.

Ethical Concern 1: Respect for Diversity and Inclusion

ACS Principle: The Primacy of the Public Interest; Enhancement of Quality of Life

Concern: Risk of cultural misrepresentation or stereotypical depiction of food traditions.

Solution: Allow user-generated content and voice recordings to preserve authenticity. Include clear moderation policies to avoid misuse while encouraging genuine storytelling and representation.

Ethical Concern 2: Informed Consent and Data Transparency

ACS Principle: Honesty; Professionalism

Concern: Users may not understand how their data or uploaded content is stored or shared.

Solution: Provide a simple, readable privacy notice that clarifies how personal stories, images, or audio files will be used. Offer consent checkboxes for submissions and a clear opt-out process.

Ethical Concern 3: Accessibility and Equal Access

ACS Principle: Enhancement of Quality of Life

Concern: Exclusion of users with disabilities or limited tech access from participation.

Solution: Follow WCAG 2.2 AA guidelines; include alt text, transcripts, keyboard navigation, and mobile responsiveness. Test across devices and provide multi-language support.

Ethical Concern 4: Misuse of Community Platform

ACS Principle: Professionalism; Public Interest

Concern: Potential for offensive content or bullying within comment/story sections.

Solution: Implement community guidelines, user flagging options, and content moderation features to ensure a safe, respectful environment.

Ethical Concern 5: Bias in AI or Automated Suggestions

ACS Principle: Professionalism; Honesty

Concern: Use of GenAI for generating personas or content may introduce cultural or gender bias.

Solution: Critically review all AI-generated outputs. Revise prompts to avoid bias, and cross-check results with diverse sources or real feedback.

Conclusion

This ethical assessment supports the creation of a respectful, inclusive, and responsible platform. All proposed solutions are rooted in the ACS Code of Ethics and aim to protect the dignity and trust of users.
