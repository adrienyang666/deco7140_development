Accessibility Audit (IA3)

The final accessibility review checks compliance with WCAG 2.2 guidelines and evaluates user flow across devices. Key features include focus indicators, responsive layouts, labelled form elements, and appropriate contrast levels.

Issue 1: Placeholder used as label

  • Element: Email address input field
  • WCAG 2.2 Criterion: 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions
  • Problem: The placeholder is the only visible label, which disappears after input starts.
  • Impact: Users with cognitive disabilities or screen readers may forget the field's purpose.
  • Fix: Add a proper <label> element linked with the input‚Äôs id.

Issue 2: Missing fieldset for form grouping

  • Element: Community Join Form
  • WCAG 2.2 Criterion: 1.3.1 Info and Relationships
  • Problem: Form inputs are not grouped semantically.
  • Impact: Screen reader users may not understand how form elements are related.
  • Fix: Wrap related inputs in a <fieldset> with a <legend>.

Issue 3: No visible focus indicator

  • Element: Buttons and navigation links
  • WCAG 2.2 Criterion: 2.4.7 Focus Visible
  • Problem: When navigating with the keyboard, there is no clear visual focus ring.
  • Impact: Keyboard users can lose track of their position, making navigation difficult.
  • Fix: Add custom :focus styles like outline: 2px solid #2d6cdf.

Response to Client: Newsletter Subscription Plug-in

We acknowledge your request to include a newsletter subscription plugin to support community engagement. We agree that providing regular updates is a valuable way to strengthen user connection with your project.

However, through our ethical analysis (A2) and accessibility audit (IA3), we identified several risks associated with third-party newsletter plug-ins. These include potential breaches of user privacy, lack of informed consent, third-party data tracking, and data being stored outside of Australia. These concerns align with principles outlined in the ACS Code of Ethics, including the primacy of the public interest (2.2), respect for user privacy (1.6), and enhancement of quality of life (1.2).

To address these concerns while still achieving your original goal, we propose a custom-built newsletter sign-up form. This form will:

  • Use a clear and accessible label with a short explanation of how data will be used
  • Include an explicit opt-in checkbox for user consent
  • Store data securely on the site‚Äôs backend, with no external tracking
  • Include a visible unsubscribe link in all emails

This approach balances ethical responsibilities with project needs. It builds user trust, reduces legal and reputational risks, and aligns with your brand‚Äôs inclusive and community-driven identity. By taking this route, your organisation demonstrates a strong commitment to ethical digital design and public interest.

We believe this solution not only supports ethical best practices but also enhances long-term user loyalty and positions your brand as a leader in accessible, trustworthy digital design.
