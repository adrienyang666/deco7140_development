⚖️ Ethical Response to Client Request

💡 Client Proposal

The client requested a third-party newsletter plug-in to improve community engagement by sending regular updates.

While the goal is valid, this request raised privacy, accessibility, and ethical concerns based on our course values and design principles.

⚠️ Risks of Third-Party Plug-ins

  • 🚫 Potential privacy breaches
  • 🌍 Data stored outside of Australia
  • 🔍 Lack of informed user consent
  • 👁️ Poor accessibility compliance

These risks contradict the ACS Code of Ethics, particularly:
1.2 – Enhance the quality of life
1.6 – Respect the privacy of others
2.2 – Prioritise the public interest

✅ Our Ethical Solution

Instead of a third-party plug-in, we designed a custom newsletter form that aligns with privacy, trust, and accessibility goals.

  • 📝 Clear and accessible labels
  • ☑️ Explicit opt-in checkbox
  • 🔐 Secure storage (no third-party tracking)
  • 📤 Unsubscribe option in every email

This method strengthens user trust while meeting the client's communication needs responsibly.

🌱 Why It Matters

Ethical digital design means protecting users and empowering them with transparent, respectful choices.

Our approach balances ethical responsibility with usability and supports your brand’s inclusive mission.

🧠 This solution demonstrates a commitment to ethical innovation, user respect, and public trust.
